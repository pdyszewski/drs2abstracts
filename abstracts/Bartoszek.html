The limit behaviour of some phylogenetic tree balance indices

Balance indices for phylogenetic trees, like Colless’s \([8]\) Sackin’s \([12]\), the cophenetic index \([10]\) or the quartet index (QI \([9]\)) admit a recursive representation that allows for finding an explicit representation of their (after scaling and centring) limit in distribution through the so–called contraction method. This method was particularly successful in studying the weak limit of the normalized number of comparisons in the Quicksort algorithm \([11]\).

Commonly the weak limits of the (scaled and centred) indices were studied for pure–birth or uniform models of tree growth (e.g. \([3,6,7]\)). However, in \([9]\) the QI was considered for much wider class of phylogenetic tree models–Aldous’s \(\beta\) model \([1,2]\). In \([4]\) we showed that the contraction method can be successfully used to characterize the weak–limit of a much wide class of indices under Aldous’s \(\beta\ge 0\) model. Namely, if for a rooted binary tree with \(n\) leaves, a normalized index, \(Y_{n}\), satisfies the recursive equation \[Y_{n} = \left(\frac{L_{n}}{n} \right)^{r} Y_{L\_{n}} + \left(1-\frac{L_{n}}{n} \right)^{r} Y_{n-L_{n}} +C_{n}(L_{n}),\] where \(L_{n}\) is the leaf clade size of one of the two daughter clades of the root, and \[C_{n}(i)=n^{-r}\left(\sum\limits_{r_{1}+r_{2}+r_{3}}C_{r_{1},r_{2},r_{3}}i^{r_{i}}(n-i)^{r_{2}}n^{r_{3}}+h_{n}(i) \right)\] is such that under our model \(\mathrm{E}[C_{n}(L_{n})]=0\), and \(\sup_{i}n^{r}h_{n}(i)\to 0\) and \(\mathrm{E}[Y_{n}^{2}]\) is uniformly bounded, then \(Y_{n}\) converges in the Wasserstein \(d\)–metric, to a random variable whose distribution is the unique fixed point of a particular transformation.

In the talk we will discuss the above result, its limit and examples of phylogenetic tree indices that fall into this framework (e.g., QI\(^{2}\) \([5]\)). The results will be illustrated with simulations.
