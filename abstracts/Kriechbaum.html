Absolute continuity of Gaussian and non-Gaussian multiplicative chaos measures

Xaver Kriechbaum (Institut de Mathémathiques de Toulouse - Université de Toulouse)

Gaussian multiplicative chaos (GMC) is a random measure introduced by Kahane \([2]\), which can be formally written as \(e^{\gamma X(x)-\frac{\gamma^2}{2}\mathrm{Var}[X(x)]}\;\mathrm{d}x\) for a centered Gaussian field \(X\). It regularly appears in the study of (asymptotically) Gaussian log-correlated fields.

We consider the non-Gaussian multiplicative chaos measure associated to the log-correlated random Fourier series, with i.i.d. coefficients \((a_k^{(i)})_{k\in\mathbb{N}, i\in\{1,2\}}\) \[S_n(t) = \sum_{k=1}^n\frac{1}{\sqrt{k}}(a_k^{(1)}\cos(2\pi k t)+a_k^{(2)}\sin(2\pi k t)),\quad t\in [0,1].\] Junnila \([1]\) has shown that \(\mu_{n,\gamma} = e^{\gamma S_n(t)}/\mathbb{E}[e^{\gamma S_n(t)}]\;\mathrm{d}t\) converges to a non-trivial limit \(\mu_\gamma\) for inverse temperature \(\gamma<\sqrt{2}\). However, it is still open which properties of GMC the random measure \(\mu_\gamma\) shares. We study this question through the lens of absolute continuity and show that for \(\gamma>1\) we can couple \(\{a_k^{(i)}\}\) with i.i.d. standard Gaussians \(\{g_k^{(i)}\}\) such that for the GMC \(M_{\gamma}\) associated to the \(g_k^{(i)}\) we have the almost sure mutual absolute continuity \[M_{\gamma}\ll \mu_{\gamma}\ll M_{\gamma}.\]

Based on joint work with Yujin H. Kim (Caltech).
